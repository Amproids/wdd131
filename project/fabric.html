Understanding Mass-Spring Systems

What are Mass-Spring Systems?

Mass-spring systems are a fundamental technique in physics simulation, used to model deformable objects like cloth, rope, and soft bodies. The system consists of a network of points (masses) connected by springs that follow Hooke's Law. When external forces like gravity or wind are applied, the system responds by calculating the forces on each mass and updating their positions accordingly.

Key Components

Mass Points

Particles with position and velocity that respond to forces.

Springs

Connections between mass points that maintain structure and transmit forces.

Forces

External influences like gravity, wind, and user interaction that affect the system.

Applications

Mass-spring systems are widely used in:

  • Video Game Physics and Animation
  • Computer Graphics and Visual Effects
  • Virtual Clothing and Fashion Design
  • Engineering Simulations
  • Interactive Digital Art
